# Mini Me — Reference Links & Inspiration

## Why Widget-First

The market is saturated with habit trackers and static-pet widgets. **No competitor owns "your day, animated on your home screen."** Mini Me's wedge: the widget reflects your real-time activity (working, exercising, eating, socializing) and friends' mini-mes appear in your scene during shared `social` blocks. This combination — activity-aware widget + ambient social presence — is uncontested.

## Art Style References

### Canonical Room Aesthetic (LOCKED)
- **PixelJoint #131408** — http://pixeljoint.com/pixelart/131408.htm
- This is THE reference for the cozy isometric pixel diorama style. Warm lamps, lived-in feel, small detailed corner of a life. Every scene we ship should feel like it could sit alongside this image.

### Isometric Pixel Rooms (Secondary Inspiration)
- **Google Images search**: "pixel rooms" — isometric pixel art room dioramas, cozy lo-fi style
  - https://www.google.com/search?q=pixel+rooms&udm=2
- **Pinterest: Isometric pixel art room** — the original reference image that anchored the isometric decision
  - https://i.pinimg.com/originals/23/9f/5a/239f5ae13db5953a196f87f864b2bb95.png
- These references show rooms viewed from a diagonal angle against dark backgrounds — the "lo-fi album cover" aesthetic

### Style Decision
- **Isometric (2.5D)** was chosen over flat 2D (Pokemon-style) and full 3D
- Isometric gives the cozy lo-fi warmth; top-down feels like a game, isometric feels like a vibe
- Every lo-fi YouTube stream thumbnail is an isometric pixel room — that's the audience overlap

### Pixel Art Asset Resources (for production)
- **itch.io**: Top-down and isometric interior asset packs — browse for reference and purchasable assets
- **TinyHouse by pixel_Salvaje**: Interactive isometric room builder (browser-based demo)
- **Penzilla's Retro Interior Pack**: 16x16 grid rooms (kitchen, bedroom, living room)
- **Envato Tuts+**: Isometric pixel art tutorials showing how rooms are built step-by-step

## Competitor References

### Direct Widget-Pet Competitors (the ones we differentiate from)

- **Pixel Pals** by Christian Selig — App Store ID `id6444085825` — https://apps.apple.com/uy/app/pixel-pets-cute-widget-app/id6444085825
  - **What they do:** Pixel art animals on home screen / lock screen / Dynamic Island
  - **What's missing:** Pet is *static* — sits there, doesn't reflect what you're doing, no social
  - **What we do better:** Widget mirrors your real-time activity; mini-me is YOU not a generic pet
- **Pixel Pet Widget — Island Pet** — App Store ID `id6445975134` — https://apps.apple.com/us/app/pixel-pet-widget-island-pet/id6445975134
  - **What they do:** Tamagotchi-style widget pet
  - **What's missing:** No activity awareness, no social presence
  - **What we do better:** same as above
- **Widgetable** — widget pets, co-parent with friends/partner
  - **What they do:** Add a partner to a Tamagotchi-style virtual pet
  - **What's missing:** It's still a pet, not "you"; the social layer is co-parenting not co-presence
  - **What we do better:** Friends' actual mini-mes appear *in your scene* during shared social blocks — ambient presence, not pet-tending
- **Finch** — virtual bird pet + self-care habits (~$2M/month revenue)
  - **What they do:** App-only self-care via a bird metaphor
  - **What's missing:** No widget presence; the bird is a pet, not the user
- **Habitica** — RPG-style habit tracker with pixel art, party system
- **Forest / Flora** — focus timer, plant trees by staying focused
- **Study Bunny** — study timer with bunny + coins (4M+ downloads)

## Key Insight from References
The Pinterest isometric room image was the moment the art direction was locked in. That specific cozy, warm, detailed pixel room diorama IS the aspirational end state users work toward. Every furniture item, every room upgrade should feel like it belongs in that image.
